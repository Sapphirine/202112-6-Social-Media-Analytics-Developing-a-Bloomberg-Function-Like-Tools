A Social Media Analytics Tool that Informs Trading Strategies

With increasing volume of retail trading in the US stock market, we have witnessed growing influence from retail trader's action on the price of particular stock. To understand this trend, we develop a Bloomberg Function like social media analytic tool to gain insight on retail investor's trading strategies, sentiments and stock of choice.

Why we are better

3 main Features

High volume, velocity, and variety data

Data used in this projects are live streaming tweets from Twitter and posts in a predefined timeframe in Subreddit r/wallstreetbets. These data are first filtered using a list of traded stock name and company's name as keywords and stored in AWS S3 buckets. Data are updated every hour to provide the most recent insights on the retail community. A sample data of Tesla related tweets contains 64000 characters in one single update.

Well trained deep learning analytics methods

In this project, we introduce a two-stage framework where in the first stage we deploy well-trained deep learning models to filter out noise data. Then in the second stage, we deploy several machine learning based methods or data visualization techniques to provide a general view of the Twitter and Reddit data, which can help us understand people's attitude towards our interested companies on social media and assist us in making investment decisions.

Highly scalable data visualization dashboard

With Amazon QuickSight, a cloud-scale business intelligence service that offers simple, actionable insights from data. We can connect to data stored in an Amazon S3 bucket and then visualize it, with a variety of data visualization options, including heat maps, histograms, KPIs, word cloud and so on.
